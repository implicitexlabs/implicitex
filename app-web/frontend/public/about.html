ImplicitEx

About

ImplicitEx is a non-custodial platform for wallet-to-wallet USDC transfers. It is being built to give users a clear, direct way to send digital value without unnecessary intermediaries, hidden fee structures, or platform custody of user funds.

The core idea is simple: a user connects their own wallet, reviews the recipient, amount, network, and fee, and submits the transaction through the ImplicitEx smart contract during the current limited live launch. ImplicitEx does not hold private keys, does not custody user assets, and does not act as a bank, broker, exchange, or wallet recovery service.

Built Around Transparent Execution

The platform fee is 1%, added on top of the transfer amount. The recipient receives the full amount you enter. For example, sending 100 USDC results in a total debit of 101 USDC, excluding network gas fees.

The fee, transfer amount, and total debit are shown before you confirm anything. No hidden spreads, no vague service charges.

Non-Custodial By Design

Users remain responsible for their own wallets, seed phrases, private keys, recipient addresses, approvals, and transaction decisions.

This reduces platform control and avoids unnecessary gatekeeping, but it also means users must review transaction details carefully.

Current Status

ImplicitEx currently operates on Polygon with a 250 USDC per-transfer limit.

Transfers have been verified on-chain. Funds move directly between wallets — ImplicitEx never holds them.

Blockchain transactions can be irreversible. A wrong address, unsupported network, compromised wallet, or user-approved transaction may result in permanent loss. ImplicitEx is being developed with that reality in mind: clear warnings, plain-language status messages, and deliberate launch gates matter.
