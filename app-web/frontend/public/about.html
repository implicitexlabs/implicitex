ImplicitEx

About

ImplicitEx is a non-custodial platform for wallet-to-wallet USDC transfers. It is being built to give users a clear, direct way to send digital value without unnecessary intermediaries, hidden fee structures, or platform custody of user funds.

The core idea is simple: a user connects their own wallet, reviews the recipient, amount, network, and fee, and submits the transaction through the ImplicitEx smart contract when live transfer execution is enabled. ImplicitEx does not hold private keys, does not custody user assets, and does not act as a bank, broker, exchange, or wallet recovery service.

Built Around Transparent Execution

The planned platform fee is a transparent 1% flat fee. That means the fee is shown before submission, separated from the amount being sent, and routed according to the contract logic when a completed transfer occurs.

The goal is not to hide cost inside confusing spreads, vague service charges, or unclear platform behavior.

Non-Custodial By Design

Users remain responsible for their own wallets, seed phrases, private keys, recipient addresses, approvals, and transaction decisions.

This reduces platform control and avoids unnecessary gatekeeping, but it also means users must review transaction details carefully.

Current Status

The current public site is a demo shell while testnet validation and transaction-flow review are completed.

Live transfers remain disabled until deployment, approval flow, execution path, negative-path testing, and production readiness gates have been reviewed and documented.

Blockchain transactions can be irreversible. A wrong address, unsupported network, compromised wallet, or user-approved transaction may result in permanent loss. ImplicitEx is being developed with that reality in mind: clear warnings, plain-language status messages, and deliberate launch gates matter.
